Mindfulness and Self-Awareness

There is a strong linear relationship between mindfulness and self awareness. As your mindfulness increases your emotional intelligence increases too. It provides clarity into your emotions and inner workings. I tried to do the body scan. It was for the most part a success, as it's pretty much like practising mindfulness, just making the focus of your mindfulness different parts of your body. My concentration wasn't perfect, but I tried. I thought the "my emotions are not me" section was very interesting. I hadn't thought about how we usually say "I am angry" instead of "I am feeling angry", and was intrigued by the discussion of viewing emotions as an experience and something you can control. I like to think that I have good control over my emotions and sort of tend to think this way already. I think it's important in order for people to keep their calm cool.

Problem Solving

During the first week when I was just starting to learn CSS I ran into a problem. I was doing an exercise in which I had to change the colour of a word to red. So I wrote what I believed the appropriate code was but it didn't work. I looked through everything again, and it seemed fine, so I tried again to no success. Refreshed the page and tried again, still nothing. I was quite frustrated by this point, so I decided to just leave it for a bit and come back. So I did and when I returned I read through the instructions again and looked at my code, and it clicked. I had written "color" the New Zealand english way instead of the American way. Which is why it took me so long to find out why it wasn't working. The error was only an error to people who use American english, so I would see it and not register any problem. It was so silly, and wasn't the only time it happened either. I can't think of an example of a problem I solve elogantly... I'll keep it in mind as we move forward though.
